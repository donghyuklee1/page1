J. Alexander Bae

Assistant Professor of Neuroscience at KAIST

I am a neuroscientist and Assistant Professor in the Department of Biological Sciences at KAIST. My research focuses on understanding the neural circuit structure underlying brain function through connectomics. By combining large-scale imaging and artificial intelligence, I build structure-based computational models that can predict circuit function and behavioral outcomes. Ultimately, my goal is to integrate spatial omics data to uncover the molecular factors defining specific neural circuit structures. I received a PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering & Neuroscience from Princeton University and a BS in Electrical Engineering from KAIST.
